Final Architecture — Run 3

  • Space-Preserving BPE Tokenizer Vocab 2048
    Regex pattern merges leading spaces into words. Achieves 3.35× compression — 128 tokens spans ~429 bytes. Trained on full 7.3MB corpus for optimal coverage.
  • 5-Layer Hybrid MoE 3 Dense + 2 MoE
    Layers 2 and 3 are MoE blocks (2 experts each). Layers 0, 1, 4 are dense — first and last always dense for embedding/output stability. Total 1.82M params, 1.49M active per token.
  • RoPE Positional Embeddings Parameter-free
    Rotary embeddings applied to Q/K projections. No learnable positional parameters wasted. Better relative position modeling vs. absolute embeddings.
  • Weight Tying Saved ~295k params
    Input embedding and LM-head weights shared. Freed capacity for a larger 2048 vocabulary at zero extra parameter cost.
  • SwiGLU Feed-Forward d_ff = 368
    LLaMA-style SwiGLU (no bias) replaces GELU. Gate, up, and down projections per expert. Smoother gradients and better convergence than standard MLP.
  • RMSNorm + AdamW + Cosine LR Modern Training Stack
    RMSNorm (learned scale only) per block. AdamW weight decay=0.1 (biases/norms excluded). Cosine decay 1e-3→1e-4 with 200-step warmup and gradient clipping (max norm 1.0).

Experiment Insights

What worked:

  • Space-preserving BPE (vocab 2048) — biggest single gain, 3.35× compression.
  • Hybrid MoE in middle layers — more total params without exceeding active cap.
  • Dense first & last layer — critical for stable gradients through embedding and output projection.
  • Weight tying — freed ~295k params for a richer vocabulary.
  • d_embd=144 with 4 heads → head_dim=36, which proved superior to 32 in attention expressiveness.

What didn't help:

  • 5→6 layers forced d_embd 144→128 (head dim 36→32), hurting attention quality more than depth helped.
  • Different MoE layer placements (runs 4 & 5) within d_embd=128 budget made no meaningful difference.
  • 50-step vs 200-step warmup had no measurable effect on final BPB.
  • Full-corpus BPE with vocab 1024 (Run 2) hurt vs 500k-corpus BPE — larger vocab budget needed to exploit full corpus.
